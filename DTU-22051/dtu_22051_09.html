Filter design in a nut shell

    Starting from s (IIR)

  • Laplace to continuous time to discrete time to z-domain: Impulse invariance method
    • \[ s \Rightarrow t \Rightarrow n \Rightarrow z \]
  • Laplace to z-domain: Bilinear transformation (TF equivalence method)
    • \[ s \Rightarrow z \]

    Starting from frequency (FIR)

  • Frequency to continuous time to discrete time (to z-domain): Impulse invariance method with windowing
    • \[ \Omega \Rightarrow t \Rightarrow n \Rightarrow z \]
  • Continuous frequency to discrete frequency to discrete time (to z-domain): Frequency domain equivalence
    • \[ \Omega \Rightarrow \omega \Rightarrow n \Rightarrow z \]

You get in charge of a car simulator platform that consists of:
  • electrodynamic shakers that simulate car vibration
  • a number of loudspeakers that simulate car acoustic

A car manufacturer comes and wants to use the platform. He/she comes with own recordings of the newest prototype and want to playback their sounds through your simulator using a playback device directly connected to your system. The problem is however that their recordings require that the frequency response of your transducers is completely flat (which is not the case by default)
